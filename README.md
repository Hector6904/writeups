# writeups

This repository documents my learning journey in cybersecurity through CTF challenges, labs, and practice rooms (such as PicoCTF, TryHackMe, HackTheBox, and others).

Unlike traditional writeups that only present the final solution, this repo focuses on the **entire problem-solving process**. Along with solutions, I include my real-time thought process, failed attempts, dead ends, and occasional bloopers. The goal is to capture how problems were actually approached and solved, not just the final answer.

## What you'll find here

* CTF challenge writeups
* Practice lab and room walkthroughs
* Notes on techniques and tools used
* Dead ends, wrong ideas, and debugging attempts
* My thought process while solving challenges

This repository acts as a **personal learning notebook** where I document what I tried, what worked, and what didn’t.

## Why this repo exists

Most writeups online show the clean final path to a solution. While those are useful, they often skip the messy exploration that happens before reaching the answer. This repository intentionally keeps that exploration.

By documenting mistakes and reasoning, I hope to:

* reinforce my own understanding
* track my progress over time
* build a personal knowledge base for future CTFs

## Structure

Writeups are organized by platform or CTF event, and then by category when relevant.

Example structure:

```
platform-or-ctf/
    challenge-name/
        writeup.md
```

## Disclaimer

These writeups reflect my personal learning process. Some solutions may differ from the intended approach or from official writeups.
